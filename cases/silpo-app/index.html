Silpo App — the consumer side of the system.

The mobile experience for Silpo loyalty members — loyalty card, scan-and-shop, store locator, bonuses. Shipped on Flutter, accessible by default, and fully consistent with the web counterpart through a shared cross-platform design system.

Context

A mobile app for millions of loyalty members.

Silpo serves millions of customers across 800+ stores. The mobile app is how loyal customers carry their relationship with the brand — bonus balance, loyalty card, store locator, in-store assistance. The expectation: native-feel, accessible, and visually coherent with the web shop.

The app and the web shop sat on different codebases — Flutter for mobile, traditional web for the shop. The challenge was to make them feel like one product without forcing the same components onto both.

Approach

Native first, system second, screens last.

  1. Native patterns firstBottom sheets, native pickers, gestures, haptics — used where users already expect them.
  2. Cross-platform DS as the spineBuilt on the Silpo Design System’s three-abstraction model — full code-reuse inside Flutter, pattern-level alignment with web.
  3. Accessibility on the foundation layerWCAG 2.1 AA contrast in the colour tokens, focus states encoded at the component level.
  4. Loyalty-first information architectureThe most-used surface for a regular customer is the loyalty card — that drove the navigation.
What I shipped

The surfaces that matter day-to-day.

  • Loyalty card & balanceThe default screen — instant access to the card, bonus balance and recent activity.
  • Scan-and-shopProduct scanner with quick add to in-store cart.
  • Store locatorMap-based search with proximity, hours and services per store.
  • Bonuses & partner programmesStatus, earn rate, history and partner-redemption flows.
  • Onboarding & authFirst-run flow optimised for one-handed phone use.
Outcomes

One product, two surfaces, one brand.

The bigger outcome was qualitative — the app stopped feeling like a separate product. Same brand voice, same components, same loyalty flow as the web. The design system did its job: it travelled.

Learnings

Three things I took from shipping consumer mobile.

  • Default screens earn their slotFor a loyalty app, the card is the home screen. Don’t make it the “Loyalty” tab.
  • Native is the default, custom is a choiceUse the picker the OS provides — until you can prove a custom one materially improves the task.
  • Accessibility on day one is cheap; on day 90 it’s a projectToken-layer contrast, focus states, motion prefs — bake them in early.
